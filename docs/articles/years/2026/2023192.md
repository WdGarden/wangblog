| If you're not really sure how to say something in English, try writing the answer to those two questions in your native language first, and then I don't want you to translate the exact words, but I do want you to translate the idea of those answers into English. | 如果你不确定某件事用英语怎么说，先用你的母语写下这两个问题的答案，然后——我不要你逐字翻译，但我要你把答案中的“意思”用英语表达出来。<br>💡 **实用技巧**：<br>- **translate the exact words**：逐字翻译（不推荐）。<br>- **translate the idea**：翻译想法（推荐）。 |
| This step can give you clarity, and it can help you avoid unnecessary details, long explanations, or going off-topic. For example, you might say, "I want to confirm the time of our meeting." Or, "I need to ask for help with a presentation." "I'm replying to a request for information." | 这一步能让你思路清晰，帮助你避免不必要的细节、冗长的解释或跑题。例如，你可能会说：“我想确认会议时间。”或者“我需要就一个演示文稿请求帮助。”“我是在回复一个信息请求。”<br>📌 **示例**：<br>- **going off-topic**：跑题。<br>- **confirm** /kənˈfɜːm/ v. 确认。 |
| **Step number two is write your email or your prompt for AI with care.** You can write the email yourself, of course, or you could use a tool like AI. Either way, this is where we have to think about that tone that we've learned about, and the structure of our email. | **第二步：用心撰写邮件或AI提示词。** 当然，你可以自己写邮件，也可以使用AI这样的工具。无论哪种方式，这里我们都需要考虑刚才学过的“语气”，以及邮件的结构。<br>✍️ **Step 2**：<br>- **with care**：用心地，仔细地。<br>- **tone** and **structure**：语气和结构。 |
| If you're writing the email yourself, keep it short and polite. Use simple, clear words and focus on just one main idea in each paragraph. | 如果你自己写邮件，保持简短和礼貌。使用简单清晰的词汇，每段只聚焦一个主要想法。<br>📝 **自写邮件建议**：<br>- **keep it short and polite**：简短且礼貌。<br>- **one main idea per paragraph**：每段一个主旨。 |
| Now, if you're going to use AI, it's important to start with a helpful prompt and as much detail as possible. For example, **write a short, polite email to a colleague asking for an update on a report.** | 如果你想用AI，重要的是从一个有用的提示词开始，并尽可能提供更多细节。例如：**给同事写一封简短礼貌的邮件，询问报告的进展。**<br>🤖 **AI提示词技巧**：<br>- **helpful prompt**：有用的提示词。<br>- **as much detail as possible**：尽可能多的细节。 |
| Then, and this is the key, **edit what AI gives you**. Take a moment to review and then adjust the tone. Remove any language that is overly formal or robotic and add your voice. | 然后，也是关键的一步，**编辑AI给出的内容**。花点时间回顾，然后调整语气。去掉任何过于正式或机器人式的语言，加入你自己的声音。<br>🔧 **关键步骤**：<br>- **edit what AI gives you**：编辑AI输出（核心！）。<br>- **add your voice**：加入你的声音。 |
| AI can give you a draft of an email, but it cannot replace your judgment or your humanity. And that brings me to step three. | AI可以给你一封邮件的草稿，但它无法取代你的判断力或你的人性。这就引出了第三步。<br>⚠️ **核心理念重申**：<br>- **judgment** /ˈdʒʌdʒmənt/ n. 判断力。<br>- **humanity** /hjuːˈmænəti/ n. 人性，人情味。 |
| **Step number three is proofread and personalize.** I always recommend reading your email out loud. This can help you hear whether the language sounds too formal or robotic. | **第三步：校对和个性化。** 我总是建议把邮件大声读出来。这能帮助你听出来语言是否听起来过于正式或像机器人。<br>🔊 **Step 3**：<br>- **proofread** /ˈpruːfriːd/ v. 校对。<br>- **personalize** /ˈpɜːsənəlaɪz/ v. 个性化。<br>- **read out loud**：大声朗读（自查语气）。 |
| And you can ask yourself these questions: Does this sound like me or does it sound like a machine? Will this feel comfortable for the person reading it, and is the tone right for our relationship? | 你可以问自己这些问题：这听起来像我，还是像机器？收信人读起来会感觉舒服吗？这个语气符合我们的关系吗？<br>🤔 **自检问题**：<br>- **sound like me**：听起来像我吗？<br>- **feel comfortable**：读起来舒服吗？<br>- **tone right for our relationship**：语气符合关系吗？ |
| After you do that and make any necessary changes, you can also add a sentence or two that shows your voice and your care. For example, "Thanks so much for your help," or, "Looking forward to hearing your thoughts. Let me know if you have any questions." | 完成这一步并做了必要修改后，你还可以加一两句体现你个人风格和关心的话。例如：“非常感谢你的帮助。”或者“期待听到你的想法。有问题随时告诉我。”<br>💌 **个性化添加**：<br>- **show your voice and care**：展现你的声音和关心。<br>- **looking forward to**：期待……。 |
| And then finally, double-check your grammar, your formatting, and your spelling. I want you to remember that AI doesn't always get it right. Once you've done those three steps, you're ready to click Send with confidence. | 最后，再检查一遍你的语法、格式和拼写。我想提醒你，AI并不总是对的。完成这三步后，你就可以自信地点击发送了。<br>✅ **发送前检查**：<br>- **double-check**：仔细复核。<br>- **with confidence**：自信地。 |
| Now, while we're talking about this, I think it's important to be clear and honest about something. Not every situation is the right time for a tool like AI. In fact, some of the most important emails you ever write are the ones where your voice, your tone, and your empathy matter the most. | 在我们讨论这个话题时，我认为有必要明确且诚实地说明一件事：并非所有情况都适合使用AI这样的工具。事实上，你写的一些最重要的邮件，恰恰是你的声音、语气和同理心最为重要的那些。<br>⚠️ **重要提醒**：<br>- **empathy** /ˈempəθi/ n. 同理心，共情能力。 |
| That includes: apologies, difficult messages or rejections, requests that require sensitivity, like feedback to a team member, or high-stakes follow-ups and conflict resolution. | 这包括：道歉、棘手的消息或拒绝、需要敏感处理的请求（如给团队成员的反馈），以及高风险的跟进和冲突解决。<br>📌 **AI不适用场景**：<br>- **difficult messages**：棘手的消息。<br>- **rejections** /rɪˈdʒekʃnz/ n. 拒绝。<br>- **sensitivity** /ˌsensəˈtɪvəti/ n. 敏感性。<br>- **high-stakes** /haɪ steɪks/ adj. 高风险的。<br>- **conflict resolution**：冲突解决。 |
| In those emails, what matters the most isn't perfect grammar, but it's how the other person feels when they read your message. And no tool can understand your relationships, your values, or your intentions the way that you do. | 在这些邮件中，最重要的不是完美的语法，而是收信人读到你的信息时感受如何。没有任何工具能像你一样理解你的人际关系、价值观或意图。<br>💖 **核心理念**：<br>- **what matters the most**：最重要的是……。<br>- **the way that you do**：像你那样（的方式）。 |
| So, if the email that you need to send is sensitive, personal, or emotional, don't delegate it. Write it yourself, or at least make sure it sounds like it is written by you and you mean every word. | 所以，如果你需要发送的邮件是敏感的、个人化的或带有情感色彩的，不要交给AI去写。自己写，或者至少确保它听起来像你写的，并且你说的每一个字都是真心实意的。<br>💬 **最终建议**：<br>- **delegate** /ˈdelɪɡeɪt/ v. 委托，交由……处理。<br>- **mean every word**：字字发自内心。 |
| I mentioned earlier that I'm definitely noticing a shift in trust as more and more emails and other things that we interact with are AI-generated, and maybe you're feeling exactly the same way. So, I think it's helpful for all of us to remember that without question, AI can help us a lot, but it's a tool, and it doesn't replace the human behind the message. | 我前面提到过，我确实注意到信任正在发生转变，因为我们接触的邮件和其他东西越来越多是AI生成的，也许你也有同感。所以，我想我们所有人都记住这一点会很有帮助：毫无疑问，AI能帮我们很多，但它是一个工具，它并不能替代信息背后的那个人。<br>🤝 **总结**：<br>- **a shift in trust**：信任的转变。<br>- **the human behind the message**：信息背后的人。 |
| Your judgment still matters. Your tone still matters. Your voice still matters. And at the end of every email is another real person, and that person deserves a message from you that feels natural, respectful, and human. | 你的判断力仍然重要。你的语气仍然重要。你的声音仍然重要。每封邮件的另一端都是另一个真实的人，那个人值得收到一封感觉自然、尊重且充满人情味的消息。<br>🎯 **核心价值重申**：<br>- **deserves** /dɪˈzɜːvz/ v. 值得，应得。<br>- **natural, respectful, and human**：自然、尊重、充满人情味。 |
| So, whether you're using AI to assist you or writing every word yourself, the goal is absolutely the same: a clear message, a confident tone, and a conversation that feels real. That's how you build trust and that's how you lead with confidence. | 所以，无论你是用AI辅助，还是自己写每一个字，目标都完全一样：清晰的信息、自信的语气、以及感觉真实的对话。这就是你建立信任的方式，这也是你自信引领的方式。<br>🏆 **结语**：<br>- **lead with confidence**：自信地引领。 |
